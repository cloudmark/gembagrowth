## Main Heading for Your Post

This is the introductory paragraph for your new blog post. Use **bold text**, which will appear in the primary green color, to emphasize key concepts. Note that all headings, at every level (e.g., `##`, `###`), will also use this green color automatically.

> This is a blockquote. Use it to highlight a key idea, a powerful quote, or a central theme from your article. It helps to break up the text and draw the reader's attention.

### A Subheading for a Deeper Dive

Here, you can expand on a specific point. This section is perfect for explaining a concept in more detail, providing evidence, or sharing a personal experience related to your topic.

You have several list styles available:

**1. Simple Bullets:** Use a hyphen (`-`) for each item. This will create a standard circular bullet point in the accent (ochre) color.

- First point or idea.
- Second point, elaborating on the first.
- A final, concluding point.

**2. Chevron Bullets:** Use `- ->-` or `- ---` for each item. This will render an ochre-colored chevron-in-a-circle icon before the text.

- ->- Is your supplier reliable or just lucky?
- --- Is your process stable or chaotic?

**3. Question Mark Bullets:** Use `- -?-` for each item. This will render an ochre-colored question mark-in-a-circle icon before the text.

- -?- A question to ponder.
- -?- Another point of inquiry.

**4. Multi-line Bullets:** For a two-line bullet, use `||` to separate the title from the description. The bolded title will appear in the primary green color. The description text will appear on a new line in a muted color.

- ->- **This is the title** || This is the description text on a new line. It will be in a muted color.

> Use another blockquote for your concluding powerful statement. This leaves a lasting impression on the reader and reinforces your main message.
